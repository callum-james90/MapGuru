About Map Guru

The Project

MapGuru is an engaging and interactive map-based geography game designed to challenge users' knowledge of world cities. In each game session, players are presented with the name of a city and a visual clue, such as an image, and must click on a world map to guess where that city is located. The closer the guess, the higher the score, encouraging both precision and learning. With a daily quiz system and a calendar feature that tracks past scores, players are motivated to return each day, improve their map skills, and challenge themselves with new cities.
The project was developed as part of a Front-End Web Development assessment for the UCD Professional Academy course. It showcases key skills such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, DOM manipulation, and API integration using Leaflet.js for the interactive map. MapGuru combines fun with education, offering a dynamic way to learn geography while demonstrating the developer's technical capabilities.


How To Play

Step 1: Click “New Game” to start a quiz for today.
Step 2: A city name and an image hint will appear above the map.
Step 3: Study the image, then click the map where you think the city is located.
Step 4: After your guess, the correct location is shown with a marker.
Step 5: You’ll see how many kilometers away your guess was and earn up to 1000 points per round.
Step 6: Each game has 5 rounds. Try to get the highest total score!
Step 7: After the game, your score is saved and shown on the calendar.
Step 8: You can only play one game per day.
Step 9: To replay past quizzes, use the calendar to pick a previous day.
Step 10:Come back tomorrow and play again.


Travel

Travel is one of the most enriching experiences in life—it opens your mind, introduces you to new cultures, and helps you see the world from different perspectives. But as exciting as it is, travel also comes with challenges, and one of the most important skills is knowing where you are. Whether you're navigating a new city, exploring remote landscapes, or using public transportation abroad, having a good sense of geography can save time, reduce stress, and keep you safe. Understanding maps, directions, and global locations not only enhances your travel experience but also boosts your confidence and independence. It allows you to appreciate the history, culture, and significance of the places you visit. In today’s world, where international travel is more accessible than ever, being geographically aware helps you connect the dots between people and places. In short, knowing where you are makes travel more meaningful, memorable, and enjoyable.
